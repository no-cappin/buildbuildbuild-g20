Here's an overview of our research project.

The Cebu-Cordova Link Expressway (CCLEX) is currently one of the longest bridges in the Philippines, which was a project under the private company Metro Pacific Tollways Corporation; the same enterprise behind other national roadways, such as the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). This started development during the leadership of former president Benigno Aquino III. However, it completed construction, and was inaugurated by former president Rodrigo Duterte. This made it a common misconception to attribute this project to his administration, as well as his Build, Build, Build program, which was Duterte's initiative of different flagship infrastructure projects all over the Philippines. The inauguration date of CCLEX, April 27, 2022, coincided with the onset of the campaign season for the 2022 Philippine general elections; thus, it has been a point of interest to analyze the association of misattributing tweets about this project during this time period. This research project aims to discover patterns on tweets that incorrectly credit this infrastructure to the Build, Build, Build campaign, with a greater objective of combating the rampant mis/disinformation plague in social media today.


Research Question

Was there a significant trend in the frequency of misattributing tweets regarding CCLEX to Duterte’s administration and the Build, Build, Build campaign before, during, and after the 2022 Philippine electoral campaign period?


Hypothesis

There was a significant uptrend in the frequency of misattributing tweets about CCLEX during the campaign period for the 2022 Philippine general elections.



Null Hypothesis

There is no significant correlation between the frequency of misattributing tweets about CCLEX and the onset of the campaign period for the 2022 Philippine general elections.


Action Plan

Analyze patterns in the number of misattributing tweets about CCLEX from three time periods: before, during, and after the campaign season for the 2022 Philippine general elections.

We scoured Twitter for misinformation.


Data Collection


TOOLS
The group collected tweets primarily through two ways: the Advanced Search feature in the Twitter website, and the automated Apify Twitter Scraper.

COLLECTION PERIOD
Data collection spanned from March 6, 2023 to April 13, 2023, while additional tweets were gathered on June 16, 2023.
Overall, a total of 165 tweets were gathered.

KEYWORDS
Using the aforementioned tools, misattributing tweets were gathered with the following keywords:
Terms related to CCLEX - cclex, #cclex, cebu cordova
Terms related to Build, Build, Build - #buildbuildbuild, buildbuildbuild, bbb
Terms related to Rodrigo Duterte - #dutertelegacy #tatakduterte, tatak duterte, prrd, duterte, digong
Terms related to giving credit - salamat, #salamatprrd, #salamatduterte, salamat duterte, salamat prrd, thanks, project

Any tweet that included any reference to Build, Build, Build were included, as CCLEX was not included as a flagship project in this campaign, thus crediting this project to BBB would have been misinformation.

Furthermore, the group only gathered misattributing tweets that were made from 2020 onwards. Only a small portion of the tweets were authored from 2016 to 2020. Thus, the inclusion of this 4-year span may skew the results, especially when performing linear regression on the frequency of tweets pre-campaign period.


Data Features


The following data features were recorded:

The credentials of the account were gathered: Account Handle, Display Name, and Account Bio.

Another data feature would be Account Type, wherein the accounts were classified into three categories:
- Anonymous, if there was no way of verifying the identities behind these accounts.
- Identified, if the account name or handle has verifiable credentials.
- Media, if the account’s primary purpose was to publish news or information in the platform.

Furthermore, other account information was obtained: Joined Date, Follower Count (the number of Twitter accounts that follow the author), Following Count (the number of Twitter accounts that the author follows), and Account Location.

Moreover, several features of the tweet were collected, such as the Tweet Content itself, the Tweet URL, and the Publication Date of the tweet.

Another feature, Tweet Type, was also recorded, if the tweet contains a text, image, video, URL. It also notes if the tweet was a reply or a quote tweet.

Content Type classifies the tweets into two categories:
- Rational, if the tweet has a purpose of being informational.
- Emotional, if the tweet states an opinion, emotion, or experience.
However, some tweets were classified as both rational and emotional, in which there were a combination of both contents in the tweet.

Lastly, the number of engagements with the tweet were also garnered: Likes, Replies, Retweets, and Quote Tweets.

Data Preprocessing


ENSURING FORMATTING CONSISTENCY
For this step, the content type of the tweets were verified. Particularly, tweets that were both rational and emotional were altered to have the same format of “Rational, Emotional” to ensure that the code will classify the tweets into one category. Additionally, the date formats of the tweets were also rechecked, and modified to have the format YY/MM/DD. This is to make it easier to sort the data upon performing linear regression.

STANDARDIZATION
Standardization was done with the tweets’ likes, retweets, replies, and quote tweets, by getting the z-score of the tweets in terms of these four features. This was done to identify possible outliers in the dataset.

HANDLING OUTLIERS
For this study, outliers were considered as tweets that attained a z-score of 3.00 and above, in terms of likes, replies, retweets, and quote tweets. Upon standardization, 3 outliers were discovered. Since this is merely a minimal percentage of the tweets gathered, the researchers decided to omit these tweets, bringing the total number of tweets in the dataset to 162.

CATEGORICAL DATA ENCODING
Additionally, certain categorical features in the dataset were converted to a quantifiable value. This is particularly seen with the tweet types feature. A value of 1 was set for tweets who included a certain media type (e.g. photo/video, URL), while a value of 0 indicates the absence of such.

HANDLING MISSING VALUES
Upon reviewing the data collected, three features were found to be inconsistent.

Views. The view count of most tweets were unavailable during data collection; thus, the researchers decided to disregard this tweet feature for the subsequent parts of the study.

Location The account locations of the tweet authors were inconsistent. Some accounts had dubious locations, while others did not provide a location in their account. Meanwhile, most accounts had the location “Philippines” or “Republic of the Philippines,” which are broad, and thus, will not provide much insight with the dataset. Thus, the researchers have also decided to dismiss this tweet feature.

Account Bio. The account bios of tweet authors were also inconsistent. Similar to account location, some accounts did not have a bio. Meanwhile, other account bios do not provide significant insight into the study, as they were unrelated to the topic. Thus, the researchers have decided to omit this tweet feature.

Statistical Hypothesis Test


The process behind the statistical testing for this project could be seen in this Google Colab file.

ASSUMPTIONS
Simple linear regression models rely on three main assumptions about the data.

The first assumption is the homogeneity of variance, which implies that the error size in the prediction remains relatively constant across different values of the independent variable. It is important to note that linear regression minimizes the root mean square error (RMSE), and thus, regions with higher error have a greater impact on the model. To address this, the dataset is segmented to prevent skewed results, and separate regression models are applied to each segment.

The second assumption is the independence of observations. This means that the dataset's observations were collected using valid sampling methods. Failing to use appropriate sampling methods introduces bias into the dataset, which may result in a hidden relationship that exists only within the sample and not within the actual population. To mitigate this, simple random sampling is employed during data collection.

The last assumption is linearity. Linearity assumes that the relationship between the independent and dependent variable is linear. If a linear relationship is not present in the dataset, nonlinear regression models are more preferred. To confirm that a linear regression model will suffice for the dataset, line graphs were manually inspected for linear and non-linear patterns during data visualization.

REGRESSION RESULTS
Pre-Campaign Period:
There is a significant relationship between the misattribution tweets of CCLEX to Duterte and Build, Build, Build program in the pre-campaign period with p-value of 7.5539692847433205x10-6, regression coefficient or R2 of 0.17, and a root mean squared error of 0.20.

During Campaign Period:
There is a significant relationship between the misattribution tweets of CCLEX to Duterte and Build, Build, Build program in the campaign period with p-value of 0.029857949629570644, regression coefficient or R2 of 0.36, and a root mean squared error of 0.22.

Post-Campaign Period:
There is a significant relationship between the misattribution tweets of CCLEX to Duterte and Build, Build, Build program in the post-campaign period with p-value of 0.0036559289265099314, regression coefficient or R2 of 0.24, and a root mean squared error of 0.17.

INTERPRETATION
The researchers reject the null hypothesis at the 5% significance level. There is sufficient evidence to support the claim that there is a significant trend of misattribution tweets of CCLEX to Duterte and Build, Build, Build program before, during, and after the May 2022 Philippine electoral campaign period.

Machine Learning Outcomes


The process behind the machine learning for this project could be seen in this Google Colab file.

EVENT DETECTION MODELS
The researchers decided to use the following machine learning models in the study: Peak Detection, Change Point Detection, and Anomaly Detection.

First, peak detection spots all local maxima by comparing its neighboring values. Moreover, subsets of peaks can be identified by setting values to the peak’s properties such as height, width, threshold, distance, and prominence.

Second, change point detection works by identifying time steps when some statistical property changes, in the case of the study, frequency. Moreover, the researchers decided to use the ruptures Python library for the offline change point detection. The detection was done via the Pelt algorithm as well as imposing a penalty value for the algorithm. The output features of this machine learning model are the number of change points as well as its corresponding dates.

Lastly, anomaly detection works by identifying data points that deviate from the dataset’s normal behavior. The unsupervised detection technique was done using isolation forests which uses a tree structure wherein data points are subsampled based on randomly selected features. Moreover, data points that end up on shorter branches indicate anomalies as these were easier for the tree to separate these from other observations.

MODEL TRAINING
Peak Detection. The researchers decided to set the parameters to the following values in peak detection: height to 5, denoting that minimum of 5 weekly tweets; width to 1, which means “breadth of a peak” is 1; threshold to 1, meaning the vertical distance between weekly tweets is set to 1; distance to 4, indicating that peaks have distance of a month since the researchers used weekly intervals; and prominence to 3, which denotes that the minimum vertical distance between the peak and its contour line.

Change-Point Detection. Meanwhile, the researchers set the penalty value of the Pelt algorithm in Change Point detection to 1.25, by means of visual inspection. The researchers opt for this value as lower penalty value led to more change points that can be caused by noise as well as lesser significance of identified change points, meanwhile setting higher penalty value might miss out relevant change points in the dataset.

Anomaly Detection. Lastly, the researchers opted for a conservative value for the contamination parameter in Anomaly detection. The researchers set the value for the contamination parameter to 0.004 which indicates the fraction of the dataset containing abnormal instances.

EVALUATION RESULTS
Misattribution tweets were found to have peaked in the weeks June 13 to 19, 2021 and April 24 to 30, 2022.

Change points were observed in misattribution tweets on several dates: May 2 and November 28, 2021; and April 17, May 22, and July 31, 2022.

Anomalous misattribution tweet frequency was found in the week April 24 to 30, 2022.

Here's what we conclude.


The plague of misinformation in the Philippines is nothing new to its society. Various cases of credit-grabbing have been notorious throughout the country’s history, serving the benefit of polishing the image of a famous politician or personality. On the other hand, time periods such as the national elections have always been an avenue for such deception to occur, where campaigning tactics could use the spread of false claims and information to gain the upper hand.

To further elaborate, the phenomenon of CCLEX’s misattribution could be perceived as political posturing by the previous administration. This, all in an effort to take advantage of the opportunity to attract voters into believing that they have fulfilled their promise of improving infrastructure in the country, hoping for a political dynasty to win the upcoming election, along with the political machinery that they have created.

Given the predicament of these two phenomena in the country, the misattribution of CCLEX is a good point of analysis. Especially with its inauguration date coinciding with the 2022 general elections, it could be possible that the trend of its misattribution could have been correlated with this time period.

Given such rationale, this research intended to find patterns among misattribution and the campaign period, through a time-analysis of mis/disinforming tweets that incorrectly credit the construction of CCLEX to the Duterte administration and/or the Build, Build, Build program.

Through the use of scrapers and Twitter search features, the group was able to gather such misattributing tweets, particularly those published from 2020 to 2022. Preliminary data visualization showed that most tweets were of purely emotional context, while anonymous Twitter accounts contributed a lion’s share of the tweets gathered.

Thereafter, the group analyzed the trend in the frequency of misattribution among three time periods: pre, during, and post-campaign election, which was done through fit-segmented linear regression. Meanwhile, machine learning was also utilized to obtain further insights through the use of three event detection models: peak, change-point, and anomaly detection.

After performing linear regression and machine learning, the findings establish a significant trend in the misattribution of CCLEX to Duterte’s administration and Build, Build, Build program before, during, and after the May 2022 electoral campaign. With this, the group discovered a significant correlation using such regression analysis, along with the detected peaks and anomalies prior to the elections, and discovered change points before and after the election.

This strengthens the association between the onset of the campaign period and the frequency of misattribution. However, this does not imply causation, as the identified peaks, change-points, and anomalies may merely coincide with particular events, such as the aforementioned inauguration, and multiple progress updates published during its construction.

Thus, the group recommends looking further into the association of misattributing tweets about CCLEX to the campaign period, particularly with the context analysis approach, to see if the sentiments were significantly related to the upcoming elections. Furthermore, the scope of future researches should also involve other social media platforms, such as Facebook and TikTok, where mis/disinformation could also be rampant.

It could also be possible to look into other projects that are commonly misattributed, as well as other coinciding political and social events that may warrant a trend of misinformation.

In conclusion, it is essential to recognize the extent of social media as a source of information nowadays, through which, the dissemination of false information could have more drastic effects on society. The results of the project justifies such sentiment more, which should serve as a reminder for people to be more meticulous with the information they receive. The presence of misattributing tweets that were classified as rational, and those that were published by media accounts are alarming, as false information takes the guise of credibility to become more believable. Individual interventions are a step in the right direction; however, the heavier involvement of more fact-checking in these platforms is even more suggested, especially during time periods such as elections, to better combat the dis/misinformation being perpetuated all over the internet.
