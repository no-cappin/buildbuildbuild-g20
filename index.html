Here's an overview of our research project.

The Cebu-Cordova Link Expressway (CCLEX) is currently one of the longest bridges in the Philippines, which was a project under the private company Metro Pacific Tollways Corporation; the same enterprise behind other national roadways, such as the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). This started development during the leadership of former president Benigno Aquino III. However, it completed construction, and was inaugurated by former president Rodrigo Duterte. This made it a common misconception to attribute this project to his administration, as well as his Build, Build, Build program, which was Duterte's initiative of different flagship infrastructure projects all over the Philippines. The inauguration date of CCLEX, April 27, 2022, coincided with the onset of the campaign season for the 2022 Philippine general elections; thus, it has been a point of interest to analyze the association of misattributing tweets about this project during this time period. This research project aims to discover patterns on tweets that incorrectly credit this infrastructure to the Build, Build, Build campaign, with a greater objective of combating the rampant mis/disinformation plague in social media today.


Research Question

Was there a significant trend in the frequency of misattributing tweets regarding CCLEX to Duterte’s administration and the Build, Build, Build campaign before, during, and after the 2022 Philippine electoral campaign period?


Hypothesis

There was a significant uptrend in the frequency of misattributing tweets about CCLEX during the campaign period for the 2022 Philippine general elections.



Null Hypothesis

There is no significant correlation between the frequency of misattributing tweets about CCLEX and the onset of the campaign period for the 2022 Philippine general elections.


Action Plan

Analyze patterns in the number of misattributing tweets about CCLEX from three time periods: before, during, and after the campaign season for the 2022 Philippine general elections.

We scoured Twitter for misinformation.


Data Collection


TOOLS
The group collected tweets primarily through two ways: the Advanced Search feature in the Twitter website, and the automated Apify Twitter Scraper.

COLLECTION PERIOD
Data collection spanned from March 6, 2023 to April 13, 2023, while additional tweets were gathered on June 16, 2023.
Overall, a total of 165 tweets were gathered.

KEYWORDS
Using the aforementioned tools, misattributing tweets were gathered with the following keywords:
Terms related to CCLEX - cclex, #cclex, cebu cordova
Terms related to Build, Build, Build - #buildbuildbuild, buildbuildbuild, bbb
Terms related to Rodrigo Duterte - #dutertelegacy #tatakduterte, tatak duterte, prrd, duterte, digong
Terms related to giving credit - salamat, #salamatprrd, #salamatduterte, salamat duterte, salamat prrd, thanks, project

Any tweet that included any reference to Build, Build, Build were included, as CCLEX was not included as a flagship project in this campaign, thus crediting this project to BBB would have been misinformation.

Furthermore, the group only gathered misattributing tweets that were made from 2020 onwards. Only a small portion of the tweets were authored from 2016 to 2020. Thus, the inclusion of this 4-year span may skew the results, especially when performing linear regression on the frequency of tweets pre-campaign period.


Data Features


The following data features were recorded:

The credentials of the account were gathered: Account Handle, Display Name, and Account Bio.

Another data feature would be Account Type, wherein the accounts were classified into three categories:
- Anonymous, if there was no way of verifying the identities behind these accounts.
- Identified, if the account name or handle has verifiable credentials.
- Media, if the account’s primary purpose was to publish news or information in the platform.

Furthermore, other account information was obtained: Joined Date, Follower Count (the number of Twitter accounts that follow the author), Following Count (the number of Twitter accounts that the author follows), and Account Location.

Moreover, several features of the tweet were collected, such as the Tweet Content itself, the Tweet URL, and the Publication Date of the tweet.

Another feature, Tweet Type, was also recorded, if the tweet contains a text, image, video, URL. It also notes if the tweet was a reply or a quote tweet.

Content Type classifies the tweets into two categories:
- Rational, if the tweet has a purpose of being informational.
- Emotional, if the tweet states an opinion, emotion, or experience.
However, some tweets were classified as both rational and emotional, in which there were a combination of both contents in the tweet.

Lastly, the number of engagements with the tweet were also garnered: Likes, Replies, Retweets, and Quote Tweets.

Data Preprocessing


ENSURING FORMATTING CONSISTENCY
For this step, the content type of the tweets were verified. Particularly, tweets that were both rational and emotional were altered to have the same format of “Rational, Emotional” to ensure that the code will classify the tweets into one category. Additionally, the date formats of the tweets were also rechecked, and modified to have the format YY/MM/DD. This is to make it easier to sort the data upon performing linear regression.

STANDARDIZATION
Standardization was done with the tweets’ likes, retweets, replies, and quote tweets, by getting the z-score of the tweets in terms of these four features. This was done to identify possible outliers in the dataset.

HANDLING OUTLIERS
For this study, outliers were considered as tweets that attained a z-score of 3.00 and above, in terms of likes, replies, retweets, and quote tweets. Upon standardization, 3 outliers were discovered. Since this is merely a minimal percentage of the tweets gathered, the researchers decided to omit these tweets, bringing the total number of tweets in the dataset to 162.

CATEGORICAL DATA ENCODING
Additionally, certain categorical features in the dataset were converted to a quantifiable value. This is particularly seen with the tweet types feature. A value of 1 was set for tweets who included a certain media type (e.g. photo/video, URL), while a value of 0 indicates the absence of such.

HANDLING MISSING VALUES
Upon reviewing the data collected, three features were found to be inconsistent.
Views. The view count of most tweets were unavailable during data collection; thus, the researchers decided to disregard this tweet feature for the subsequent parts of the study.
Location The account locations of the tweet authors were inconsistent. Some accounts had dubious locations, while others did not provide a location in their account. Meanwhile, most accounts had the location “Philippines” or “Republic of the Philippines,” which are broad, and thus, will not provide much insight with the dataset. Thus, the researchers have also decided to dismiss this tweet feature.
Account Bio. The account bios of tweet authors were also inconsistent. Similar to account location, some accounts did not have a bio. Meanwhile, other account bios do not provide significant insight into the study, as they were unrelated to the topic. Thus, the researchers have decided to omit this tweet feature.

ASSUMPTIONS
Simple linear regression models rely on three main assumptions about the data.
The first assumption is the homogeneity of variance, which implies that the error size in the prediction remains relatively constant across different values of the independent variable. It is important to note that linear regression minimizes the root mean square error (RMSE), and thus, regions with higher error have a greater impact on the model. To address this, the dataset is segmented to prevent skewed results, and separate regression models are applied to each segment.
The second assumption is the independence of observations. This means that the dataset's observations were collected using valid sampling methods. Failing to use appropriate sampling methods introduces bias into the dataset, which may result in a hidden relationship that exists only within the sample and not within the actual population. To mitigate this, simple random sampling is employed during data collection.
The last assumption is linearity. Linearity assumes that the relationship between the independent and dependent variable is linear. If a linear relationship is not present in the dataset, nonlinear regression models are more preferred. To confirm that a linear regression model will suffice for the dataset, line graphs were manually inspected for linear and non-linear patterns during data visualization.
